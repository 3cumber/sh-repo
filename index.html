Shawarma Compass points you toward the nearest shawarma and keeps the name a secret until you are practically standing in front of it.

How it works

Your phone's GPS finds where you are and its compass finds which way you face. The arrow combines both to point at the closest spot. The name stays hidden until you are within your reveal distance, at which point the visit is logged and you can leave a review.

Where the spots come from

Nearby places are pulled live from OpenStreetMap. Coverage and tagging vary, so if something is missing use the “Log a spot” button to add it yourself.

Map data © OpenStreetMap contributors, available under the Open Database License.

Privacy

Everything stays on this device. There is no account and no server. Your location is never sent anywhere except to OpenStreetMap to look up nearby places. Use Export to keep a backup, since clearing your browser data will erase your progress.

Good to know

The compass needs a real phone over HTTPS to work properly. On Android the heading is best-effort. The “Open in Maps” link only carries coordinates, but the map itself will still show the place once it loads.
