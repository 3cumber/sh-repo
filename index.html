Shawarma Compass points you toward the nearest shawarma and keeps the name a secret until you are practically standing in front of it.

How it works

Your phone's GPS finds where you are and its compass finds which way you face. The arrow combines both to point at the closest spot. The name stays hidden until you are within your reveal distance, at which point the visit is logged and you can leave a review.

Where the spots come from

Nearby places are pulled live from OpenStreetMap. If something is missing use the "Log a spot" button. Map data © OpenStreetMap contributors, ODbL license.

Privacy

Everything stays on this device. No account, no server. Use Export to back up your progress.

Good to know

The compass needs a real phone over HTTPS. On Android the heading is best-effort. "Open in Maps" only carries coordinates.
